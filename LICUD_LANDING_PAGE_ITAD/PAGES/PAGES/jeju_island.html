Jeju Island

   It's known for its beach resorts and volcanic landscape of craters and cavelike lava tubes. Hallasan Mountain, a dormant volcano, features hiking trails, a crater lake at the 1,950m summit and nearby Gwaneumsa Temple.


   There are two ways to get to Jeju Island: (1) Turn your trip into a multi-city tour by planning a stopover in Hong Kong, which has nonstop flights to Jeju Island via Cathay Pacific, Asiana Airlines, China Eastern Airlines, China Southern Airlines, Air Busan, and Hong Kong Express. (2) Large travel agencies like Rakso Travel include chartered flights via Philippine Airlines between Manila and Jeju Island in their packages.


   Not only Jeju Island is South Korea's landmark, there are other tourist spots that everyone can go to like N Seoul Tower, Myeong-dong, Bukchon Hanok Village, etc.
